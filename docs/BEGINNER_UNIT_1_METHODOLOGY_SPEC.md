# Beginner Unit 1 Methodology Spec

## Unit Identity

- Course: Beginner English for Adults
- Unit: Unit 1. Nice to meet you
- Status: v0.2 methodology preparation
- Content policy: original ULC material only
- Student UI and instructions: Russian
- Learning content: English

## Unit Goal

Студент Beginner может представиться, назвать имя, страну/город, профессию, телефон/email, задать простые вопросы, понять короткий диалог знакомства и поддержать минимальный разговор при первой встрече.

## Methodology Guardrails

- The unit follows the structure and classroom rhythm of a modern communicative adult English course: lead-in, clear language input, controlled practice, listening/dialogue work, pronunciation, semi-controlled speaking, practical speaking outcome, homework.
- The unit must not copy English File / New English File texts, tasks, dialogues, characters, illustrations, audio, workbook pages, or design.
- Every lesson ends in a speaking outcome.
- Language load stays Beginner-friendly: short chunks, high repetition, visible support, no grammar overload.
- Russian is used for instructions and explanations; English is used for examples, target phrases, dialogues, prompts, and student output.

## Unit Language Spine

- Greetings and introductions: `Hello`, `Hi`, `I'm ...`, `Nice to meet you`.
- `be`: `I am`, `I'm`, `you are`, `are you`.
- Countries, cities, nationalities at recognition level.
- Jobs with `a/an`.
- Numbers 0-20, phone numbers, spelling names.
- Personal information: name, country, city, job, phone, email.
- Practical first-meeting dialogue.

## Lesson 1: Hello! I'm...

### Lesson Goal

Студент понимает и использует базовые приветствия, называет имя и реагирует на знакомство.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can say: `Hello. I'm Anna. Nice to meet you.`

### Target Vocabulary

- `hello`, `hi`, `good morning`, `good evening`
- `name`
- `nice`, `meet`, `you`, `too`

### Target Grammar

- `I am ...`
- contraction: `I'm ...`
- fixed chunk: `Nice to meet you.`

### Pronunciation Focus

- Sentence stress in `I'm Anna`.
- Linked rhythm in `Nice to meet you`.
- Clear final sound in `meet`.

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original short dialogue:

```text
A: Hello. I'm Dima.
B: Hi, Dima. I'm Kate.
A: Nice to meet you.
B: Nice to meet you, too.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read name cards.
- Write one mini-introduction: `Hello. I'm ...`

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: teacher shows 3 greetings and students choose formal/informal.
2. Listen and repeat: short dialogue with pauses.
3. Language focus: `I am` vs `I'm`.
4. Controlled practice: complete greeting chunks.
5. Pair speaking: exchange names with 3 classmates.
6. Exit speaking: record or say one introduction.

### Homework Flow

- Review greetings.
- Complete 5 short controlled tasks.
- Write and rehearse a 2-line self-introduction.

### Exercise List

- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Впишите пропущенное слово." Content: `Hello. ___ Anna.`
- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите лучший ответ." Content: `A: Nice to meet you. B: ...`
- Matching: Инструкция: "Соедините фразу и значение." Content: `Hello`, `Nice to meet you`, `too`.
- Word order: Инструкция: "Соберите фразу." Content: `I'm / Anna`.
- Listen and choose: Инструкция: "Прослушайте mock-аудио и выберите имя." Content: `I'm Kate.`
- Short writing: Инструкция: "Напишите 2 короткие фразы о себе." Content: `Hello. I'm ...`

### Teacher Notes

- Keep pace slow and confident; avoid explaining all forms of `be`.
- Drill chunks as whole phrases before showing grammar.
- Encourage students to stand up for pair greetings if class format allows.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- Omitting `am`: `I Anna`.
- Saying `I am meet you`.
- Translating word by word instead of using chunk.
- Pronouncing `meet` too short or unclear.

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `I Anna`. Explanation: "В английском нужно маленькое слово am: I am Anna или коротко I'm Anna."
- Ошибка: `Nice meet you`. Explanation: "В этой фразе нужен to: Nice to meet you. Запоминайте как готовый chunk."

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student chooses the correct reply to `Nice to meet you`.
- Student completes `I'm ...` with their name.
- Student can say a 2-line introduction without reading every word.
- Teacher can see common errors: missing `am`, missing `to`.

## Lesson 2: Where are you from?

### Lesson Goal

Студент спрашивает и отвечает, откуда он или другой человек.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can ask and answer: `Where are you from?` - `I'm from Minsk.`

### Target Vocabulary

- `country`, `city`
- selected countries/cities relevant to adult learners: `Belarus`, `Poland`, `Lithuania`, `Minsk`, `Warsaw`, `Vilnius`
- `from`

### Target Grammar

- `Where are you from?`
- `I'm from ...`
- `Are you from ...?` - `Yes, I am.` / `No, I'm not.`

### Pronunciation Focus

- Weak form of `are` in `Where are you from?`
- Stress on place name: `I'm from Minsk`.

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original short dialogue:

```text
A: Hi, I'm Lena.
B: Hello, Lena. I'm Pavel.
A: Where are you from?
B: I'm from Grodno. And you?
A: I'm from Minsk.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read simple profile cards with name and city.
- Write one profile line: `I'm from ...`

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: map/card prompt with cities.
2. Listen for gist: choose city names from the dialogue.
3. Grammar focus: question order `Where + are + you + from`.
4. Controlled practice: gap-fill and word order.
5. Pair speaking: ask 4 people and note answers.
6. Speaking outcome: introduce partner: `This is ... He/She is from ...` as optional teacher stretch.

### Homework Flow

- Complete question-order practice.
- Match countries/cities.
- Write 3 answers with `I'm from ...`

### Exercise List

- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Впишите слово." Content: `I'm ___ Minsk.`
- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите вопрос." Content: `Where are you from?`
- Matching: Инструкция: "Соедините city/country." Content: `Minsk - city`, `Belarus - country`.
- Drag-and-drop: Инструкция: "Соберите ответ." Content: `I'm / from / Belarus`.
- Word order: Инструкция: "Соберите вопрос." Content: `Where / are / you / from`.
- Listen and type: Инструкция: "Напечатайте услышанную фразу." Content: `I'm from Vilnius.`

### Teacher Notes

- Drill question as one sound group before showing word order.
- Accept city or country in answers.
- Do not introduce many nationalities yet; recognition only if needed.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- `Where you from?`
- `I from Minsk.`
- Confusing city and country.
- Overusing `am` in question: `Where am you from?`

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `Where you from?` Explanation: "В вопросе нужно are: Where are you from?"
- Ошибка: `I from Minsk`. Explanation: "После I нужен глагол am: I am from Minsk или I'm from Minsk."

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student asks `Where are you from?`
- Student answers with `I'm from ...`
- Student identifies city/country in a short dialogue.
- Student completes at least one correct question-order exercise.

## Lesson 3: What's your job?

### Lesson Goal

Студент называет простую профессию и спрашивает о профессии собеседника.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can say: `I'm a manager.` and ask: `What's your job?`

### Target Vocabulary

- `manager`, `teacher`, `driver`, `doctor`, `student`, `engineer`, `designer`, `administrator`
- `job`, `work`

### Target Grammar

- `I'm a/an ...`
- `What's your job?`
- `Are you a/an ...?`
- `a` vs `an` before jobs.

### Pronunciation Focus

- Stress in common jobs: `manager`, `designer`, `engineer`.
- Natural rhythm: `What's your job?`

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original short dialogue:

```text
A: What's your job?
B: I'm an engineer.
A: Oh, nice. Are you from Minsk?
B: No, I'm from Brest.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read simple job badges.
- Write: `I'm a/an ... I'm from ...`

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: students guess jobs from simple icons or words.
2. Vocabulary input: 8 adult-relevant jobs.
3. Grammar focus: `a/an` before jobs.
4. Controlled practice: choose `a` or `an`.
5. Listening: identify job and city.
6. Speaking: mini-networking, ask name/city/job.

### Homework Flow

- Match jobs and pictures/definitions.
- Complete `a/an` practice.
- Write a 3-line profile.

### Exercise List

- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите правильный артикль." Content: `I'm ___ engineer.`
- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Впишите профессию." Content: `I'm a ___.`
- Matching: Инструкция: "Соедините job и русский перевод." Content: `teacher`, `driver`, `manager`.
- Word order: Инструкция: "Соберите вопрос." Content: `What's / your / job`.
- Listen and choose: Инструкция: "Прослушайте mock-диалог и выберите профессию." Content: `I'm a designer.`
- Short writing: Инструкция: "Напишите 3 строки о себе." Content: `I'm ... I'm from ... I'm a/an ...`

### Teacher Notes

- Keep jobs limited; add student jobs only if they are easy and useful.
- Correct `a/an` gently; speaking confidence is more important than perfect article control.
- Use partner cards for controlled repetition.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- Omitting article: `I'm manager`.
- Using `an` before consonant sound: `an teacher`.
- Saying `What your job?`
- Pronouncing `engineer` with stress on the first syllable.

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `I'm manager`. Explanation: "Перед профессией в единственном числе нужен a или an: I'm a manager."
- Ошибка: `What your job?` Explanation: "В вопросе нужен is в короткой форме: What's your job?"

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student uses `I'm a/an ...` with at least one job.
- Student asks `What's your job?`
- Student chooses `a/an` correctly in controlled examples.
- Student can make a 3-line oral profile.

## Lesson 4: Numbers, Phone Numbers and Spelling

### Lesson Goal

Студент понимает и произносит числа 0-20, диктует телефон и spell-ит имя/email на базовом уровне.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can say: `My phone number is ...` and spell a simple name/email.

### Target Vocabulary

- numbers `zero` to `twenty`
- `phone number`, `email`, `letter`, `spell`
- alphabet letters needed for common names/emails

### Target Grammar

- `My phone number is ...`
- `My email is ...`
- `How do you spell it?`

### Pronunciation Focus

- `thirteen` vs `thirty` is recognition only; production focuses on 0-20.
- Stress in `thirteen`, `fourteen`, `fifteen`.
- Letter sounds: `A`, `E`, `I`, `Y`.

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original short dialogue:

```text
A: What's your phone number?
B: It's 29 14 08.
A: How do you spell your name?
B: A-N-N-A.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read contact cards.
- Write one phone number and one simple email.

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: number recognition game.
2. Listen and repeat: numbers 0-20.
3. Controlled practice: choose heard number.
4. Functional language: `How do you spell it?`
5. Pair dictation: phone numbers and names.
6. Speaking outcome: exchange contact details in a role-play.

### Homework Flow

- Listen-and-choose number practice.
- Type short phone/email details.
- Prepare one contact card.

### Exercise List

- Listen and choose: Инструкция: "Выберите услышанное число." Content: `seventeen`.
- Listen and type: Инструкция: "Напечатайте номер." Content: `14 09`.
- Matching: Инструкция: "Соедините число и слово." Content: `12 - twelve`.
- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Впишите слово." Content: `My phone ___ is 29 14 08.`
- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите правильный вопрос." Content: `How do you spell it?`
- Short writing: Инструкция: "Напишите contact card." Content: `Name`, `Phone`, `Email`.

### Teacher Notes

- Do not overload with full alphabet mastery; focus on practical spelling.
- Model phone numbers in pairs, not as long digit strings.
- Use slow teacher audio first, then natural speed.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- Confusing `thirteen` and `three`.
- Saying phone digits too fast.
- Mispronouncing letters `E` and `I`.
- Using Russian word order in `My email is ...`

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `My phone is 29 14 08`. Explanation: "Так можно понять, но точнее: My phone number is 29 14 08."
- Ошибка: `How spell it?` Explanation: "В вопросе нужен do: How do you spell it?"

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student recognizes numbers 0-20 in simple tasks.
- Student says a phone number in pairs of digits.
- Student asks `How do you spell it?`
- Student writes a simple contact card.

## Lesson 5: Personal Information

### Lesson Goal

Студент объединяет имя, город/страна, профессию, телефон/email в короткую анкету и мини-разговор.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can ask and answer simple personal information questions.

### Target Vocabulary

- `first name`, `last name`, `country`, `city`, `job`, `phone number`, `email`
- review: greetings, countries/cities, jobs, numbers

### Target Grammar

- Review: `I'm ...`, `I'm from ...`, `I'm a/an ...`
- Questions: `What's your name?`, `Where are you from?`, `What's your job?`, `What's your phone number?`

### Pronunciation Focus

- Rising intonation in yes/no questions: `Are you from Minsk?`
- Falling intonation in wh-questions: `What's your job?`

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original information-form dialogue:

```text
A: What's your first name?
B: Nina.
A: What's your job?
B: I'm a teacher.
A: What's your email?
B: nina.ulc@example.com.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read a simple registration form.
- Complete a personal information form.
- Write a 4-line personal profile.

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: identify fields on a simple form.
2. Review language: name, from, job, phone, email.
3. Listening: complete missing form details.
4. Controlled practice: choose correct question for each field.
5. Pair speaking: interview and complete partner form.
6. Speaking outcome: introduce partner using the form.

### Homework Flow

- Complete a personal information form.
- Correct typical mistakes in questions.
- Record or write a short self-profile.

### Exercise List

- Matching: Инструкция: "Соедините поле формы и вопрос." Content: `First name - What's your first name?`
- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Впишите пропущенное слово." Content: `What's your ___ number?`
- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите правильный вопрос." Content: `What's your job?`
- Drag-and-drop: Инструкция: "Соберите профиль." Content: `I'm / from / Minsk`.
- Listen and type: Инструкция: "Заполните поле из mock-аудио." Content: `nina.ulc@example.com`.
- Short writing: Инструкция: "Напишите 4 строки о себе." Content: `Name`, `From`, `Job`, `Contact`.

### Teacher Notes

- Treat forms as speaking support, not paperwork.
- Keep privacy safe: use mock phone/email if students prefer.
- Encourage full chunks, not isolated one-word answers.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- `What is your phone?` instead of `What's your phone number?`
- Missing `your` in questions.
- Writing email aloud with Russian letter names.
- Confusing `first name` and `last name`.

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `What your name?` Explanation: "В вопросе нужен is: What's your name?"
- Ошибка: `I from Belarus, manager`. Explanation: "Сделайте две короткие фразы: I'm from Belarus. I'm a manager."

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student completes a simple personal information form.
- Student asks at least 4 personal information questions.
- Student answers in short complete chunks.
- Student can introduce a partner using form notes.

## Lesson 6: Practical English: Meeting a New Person

### Lesson Goal

Студент собирает материал Unit 1 в практический диалог первой встречи.

### Speaking Outcome

Student can hold a short first-meeting conversation: greeting, name, city/country, job, contact detail, polite close.

### Target Vocabulary

- review: greetings, names, countries/cities, jobs, numbers, spelling, contact details
- polite chunks: `Great`, `Thanks`, `See you`, `Have a good day`

### Target Grammar

- Review: `I'm ...`, `I'm from ...`, `I'm a/an ...`
- Review questions: `What's your ...?`, `Where are you from?`, `Are you ...?`, `How do you spell it?`

### Pronunciation Focus

- Conversation rhythm and turn-taking.
- Polite intonation in short replies.
- Clear chunking: `Nice to meet you` / `See you`.

### Listening / Dialogue Focus

Original practical dialogue:

```text
A: Hi. I'm Max.
B: Hello, Max. I'm Alina.
A: Nice to meet you, Alina. Where are you from?
B: I'm from Minsk. What about you?
A: I'm from Gomel. What's your job?
B: I'm a designer.
A: Great. How do you spell your email?
B: A-L-I-N-A dot work at example dot com.
```

### Reading / Writing Support

- Read a model dialogue.
- Complete a dialogue with missing chunks.
- Write a 6-line personal meeting dialogue.

### Classwork Flow

1. Lead-in: students order dialogue cards.
2. Listening for gist: identify people, city, job, contact.
3. Controlled practice: complete missing chunks.
4. Pronunciation: rehearse dialogue with natural pauses.
5. Semi-controlled speaking: use role cards.
6. Final speaking task: meet a new person and complete a contact note.

### Homework Flow

- Review Unit 1 phrases.
- Complete mixed practice checkpoint.
- Write and rehearse one first-meeting dialogue.
- Optional speaking widget task: record the dialogue with prompts.

### Exercise List

- Multiple choice: Инструкция: "Выберите лучшую реплику в диалоге." Content: `Nice to meet you, too.`
- Fill gap: Инструкция: "Вставьте слово." Content: `Where are you ___?`
- Word order: Инструкция: "Соберите вопрос." Content: `What's / your / job`.
- Drag-and-drop: Инструкция: "Соберите диалог по порядку." Content: greeting, name, from, job, contact.
- Listen and choose: Инструкция: "Выберите город из mock-диалога." Content: `I'm from Gomel.`
- Listen and type: Инструкция: "Напечатайте услышанный email chunk." Content: `alina.work@example.com`
- Short writing: Инструкция: "Напишите 6 строк диалога первой встречи." Content: first meeting dialogue.

### Teacher Notes

- This is a practical consolidation lesson, not a grammar test.
- Let students use support cards first, then repeat with less support.
- Use pair rotation for fluency, then one confident final attempt.
- Mark success by communication, not by perfect accuracy.

### Typical Mistakes for Russian-Speaking Beginner Students

- Forgetting question word order under speaking pressure.
- Mixing `I'm from` and `I'm a/an`.
- Reading instead of speaking.
- Over-translating polite chunks.
- Avoiding contact detail spelling because of alphabet anxiety.

### AI Explanation Examples

- Ошибка: `Where you from?` Explanation: "В живом диалоге всё равно нужен are: Where are you from?"
- Ошибка: `I'm a Minsk`. Explanation: "Minsk - это город. Для города используем from: I'm from Minsk. Для профессии: I'm a designer."

### Acceptance Criteria

- Student completes a first-meeting dialogue with at least 6 turns.
- Student asks at least 3 questions from Unit 1.
- Student gives name, city/country, job, and one contact detail.
- Student understands a short first-meeting dialogue and identifies key information.
- Student can repeat the dialogue with reduced support.

- Student can introduce themselves with name and greeting.
- Student can ask and answer where they are from.
- Student can state a simple job with `a/an`.
- Student can understand and say numbers 0-20 in contact contexts.
